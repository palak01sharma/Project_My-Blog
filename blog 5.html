Aryabhata spacecraft

Posted by Palak Sharma on June 12, 2021

The Aryabhata spacecraft, named after the famous Indian astronomer, was India's first satellite; it was completely designed and fabricated in India and launched by a Soviet Kosmos-3M rocket from Kapustin Yar on April 19, 1975.


To go places and do things that have never been done before – that’s what living is all about.

The satellite's image appeared on the reverse of Indian two rupee banknotes between 1976 and 1997

This satellite was one of the earliest ones to use CMOS devices on a large scale. The checkout system used Indian made TDC-12 computer with punched tape input/output devices.
